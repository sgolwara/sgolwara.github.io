---
title: Lion Cub
date: 2025-02-20
---

<div class = "stanza">
I<br>
Through the amber grass he stood, the heir of roaring pride,<br>
With bloodied claws and battle scars, yet never once he cried.<br>
His kin lay torn, their spirits high, though bodies met the dust,<br>
Yet in his gaze, defiance burned—a fire fierce and just.<br>
Jai Jagdamba!<br>
</div>

<div class = "stanza">
II<br>
They dragged him through the jackal’s den, his brother by his side,<br>
A bond unbroken, forged in war, where love and duty tied.<br>
Yet through the night, the traitors struck, they tore his friend away,<br>
And in the dark, the cub stood tall, alone but not astray.<br>
Jai Jagdamba!<br>
</div>

<div class = "stanza">
III<br>
They split his paws, they tore his flesh, the jackals bared their teeth,<br>
And over wounds, they poured the brine, yet still, he grinned beneath.<br>
His talons ripped, his body raw, yet not a whimper came,<br>
For every tear of blood he shed, he whispered not their name.<br>
Jai Jagdamba!<br>
</div>

<div class = "stanza">
IV<br>
"Look away!" the jackals screamed, yet still, he met their gaze,<br>
An ember bright, an unbowed soul, a sun amidst the haze.<br>
So struck with fear, they doused the flames, they gouged his eyes to blind,<br>
Yet even in the blackest void, his vision stayed unkind.<br>
Jai Jagdamba!<br>
</div>

<div class = "stanza">
V<br>
"Kneel and live!" the jackals howled, "Submit and we shall spare!"<br>
He bared his fangs and laughed aloud—a lion does not scare.<br>
"Bend to us or lose your tongue, your words will strike no more!"<br>
So silent now, yet never weak—his silence was a roar.<br>
Jai Jagdamba!<br>
</div>

<div class = "stanza">
VI<br>
Upon the rock his body lay, the dawn caressed his spine,<br>
A bloodied king, yet standing tall, his soul untouched, divine.<br>
For in the winds, his whispers sang, in warriors yet to be,<br>
A lion slain, yet never lost—his roar shook the sea.<br>
Jai Jagdamba!<br>
</div>
