---
title: Lion Cub
date: 2025-02-20
---

<div class = "stanza">
I
Through the amber grass he stood, the heir of roaring pride,
With bloodied claws and battle scars, yet never once he cried.
His kin lay torn, their spirits high, though bodies met the dust,
Yet in his gaze, defiance burned—a fire fierce and just.
Jai Jagdamba!
</div>

<div class = "stanza">
II
They dragged him through the jackal’s den, his brother by his side,
A bond unbroken, forged in war, where love and duty tied.
Yet through the night, the traitors struck, they tore his friend away,
And in the dark, the cub stood tall, alone but not astray.
Jai Jagdamba!
</div>

<div class = "stanza">
III
They split his paws, they tore his flesh, the jackals bared their teeth,
And over wounds, they poured the brine, yet still, he grinned beneath.
His talons ripped, his body raw, yet not a whimper came,
For every tear of blood he shed, he whispered not their name.
Jai Jagdamba!
</div>

<div class = "stanza">
IV
"Look away!" the jackals screamed, yet still, he met their gaze,
An ember bright, an unbowed soul, a sun amidst the haze.
So struck with fear, they doused the flames, they gouged his eyes to blind,
Yet even in the blackest void, his vision stayed unkind.
Jai Jagdamba!
</div>

<div class = "stanza">
V
"Kneel and live!" the jackals howled, "Submit and we shall spare!"
He bared his fangs and laughed aloud—a lion does not scare.
"Bend to us or lose your tongue, your words will strike no more!"
So silent now, yet never weak—his silence was a roar.
Jai Jagdamba!
</div>

<div class = "stanza">
VI
Upon the rock his body lay, the dawn caressed his spine,
A bloodied king, yet standing tall, his soul untouched, divine.
For in the winds, his whispers sang, in warriors yet to be,
A lion slain, yet never lost—his roar shook the sea.
Jai Jagdamba!
</div>
