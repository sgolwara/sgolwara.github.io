---
title: The Edge of Listening
date: 2025-03-21
---


"Listen," I heard, the sound pulling me back to reality. Five of us were huddled in this cabin in the woods. We had come here to escape our hectic city lives, to breathe air that wasn’t laced with smog and static.

Through the crack in the door, a silhouette was faintly visible. A man in tattered clothes.

"Lenore, he's going to tell us a story! What're you all spaced out for?" Elliot's voice cut through the air, his eyes narrowed at me.

"Sorry," I muttered, shaking my head. "Thought I saw something."

Elias coughed, drawing our attention. He was a historian by profession, and his stories had always been rich with the weight of things long past. Tonight, in this 
overgrown log cabin, was no different.

He took a deep breath and spoke:

"Step by step, the dancer sways,
A hollow face, an empty gaze.
No breath to take, no song to sing,
It only moves when pulled by strings."

His voice was steady, but something about the way he spoke sent a shiver down my spine.

"A mask of white, a tilted head,
A thing that walks but once was dead.
Its limbs too light, its feet don’t fall
It does not walk. It does not crawl."
His fingers traced the rim of his tin cup in a choppy, repetitive motion. A faint ringing sound filled the air.

"A lonely tune, a hollow hymn,
A whisper sung in darkened limbs.
No lips to part, no voice to speak
It hums for those who dare to sleep."

The fire crackled, its heat suddenly distant. Silence settled like mist.

There it was again, just beyond the window. Too far to see clearly, but there. Watching.

I blinked. Gone. The trees and the dark sky stared back at me.

"The watchers live, the listeners go,
A final step, a final show.
It takes them light, it takes them thin,
And pulls the strings beneath their skin."

A prickle of unease crawled up my spine. Something felt wrong, like a breath caught 
in the throat of the world.

Elias sighed, his breath heavy, his voice growing drowsy.

"It doesn’t come for those who see...."

His eyelids drooped, then fell shut. He leaned back, sinking into the wood of the chair.

A sound. A rustling. My eyes snapped open.

The embers of the fire had faded to nothing. The air hung heavy, thick, unnatural.

"Mmm mm mmh."

A hum.

I tried to sit up, but my body was sluggish.

"Mmm mm mmh."

The sound vibrated in my bones.

"Mmm mm mmh."

A second hum joined the first. One outside, one inside.

I forced my head to turn. The movement was slow, strained.

"Mmm mm mmh."

Elias.

His body was stiff, his head tilted forward. The hum spilled from his lips.

"Mmm mm mmh."

He rose. Limbs moving like they had never belonged to him. His head snapped up, followed by his hands
dead weight turned to life.

"Mmm mm mmh."

Outside the window.

The figure stood there, its clothes swaying in the wind, head tilted just so. Black eyes hollow in a porcelain mask. Watching.

"Mmm mm mmh."

It moved. Elias moved. The same motion, step for step, limb for limb.

"Mmm mm mmh."

His feet scraped against the wooden floor, jerking forward.

"Mmm mm mmh."

The hum slithered through my skull, coiling tight, drowning thought.

"Mmm mm mmh."

A song. Wordless, but absolute.

"Mmm mm mmh."

My limbs grew light. My fingers twitched. A rhythm, slow, insistent.

"Mmm mm mmh."

Elias’ lips parted. He inhaled—sharp, ragged, like the first breath of something new.

"Mmm mm mmh."

His voice joined the hum, shaping words.

"Step by step, the dancer sways...."

My foot moved forward.

"Mmm mm mmh."
