---
title: Chosen to End
date: 2026-01-22
---

I’m sorry.  

I’m sorry I have to do this to you.  

The One Who Whispers said it’s the only way. The only way to make it stop.  

I don’t know what it wants. I don’t know why I was chosen.  

But I understand what I am now.  

I am the seed of destruction for this planet.  

Like many before it, Earth, too, will fall to the One Who Whispers.  

I still remember the first time it spoke to me. It told me I was finally of age—finally mature enough to bring forth the force that devours everything we know of life and the universe. I didn’t understand then. But now… now I do.  

Ever since that night, I’ve had visions. Places I’ve never been. Faces I’ve never seen. And every single one of them dies. I’d wake to news reports confirming what I had already witnessed in my dreams. At first, the incidents were small. A fire here. A drowning there. A lone, inexplicable death in a city miles away.  

But tonight was different.  

I remember life before the visions. I had a dog. A husky. Scotty Junior. His energy was boundless. If he were here today, he would’ve powered the entire country. 
My boy was taken from me.
The night I had my first vision, he vanished.
No barking. No collar. No body.
I told myself it was just a coincidence. That maybe the gate was open. That maybe, just maybe, he found a better home.

But I know what I saw that night.
The first vision.
I saw him. I saw him die.
The vision said I killed him.
But that wasn’t me.
It couldn’t be.
I wouldn’t hurt my poor baby.
These visions didn’t just haunt me. They took everything I loved. One piece at a time.
They took my life.
They took who I was.

I woke up in a cold sweat just thirty minutes ago. The One Who Whispers was waiting. Its voice slithered through my mind, hissing with amusement.  

Then, for the first time, I saw it.  

A shadow took shape in the corner of my room, warping, twisting, pulling itself together like something being born from the dark. It came with dense, wet sounds- like tendons snapping, one by one. With each snap there was a tremor. No, that was me. Trembling. Out of fear? Probably. My nose was filled with a sickly-sweet stench, the kind which overripe fruits give off. The air was thick. I felt like I was suffocating- no, drowning. Was it the air? Or did I give in to despair? I don’t know. I don’t think I want to know. My arms wrapped around myself as I slowly looked where the shadow was.

A towering beast stood before me, a hairy, humanoid monstrosity. Its head was the skull of some goat-like creature, hollow sockets leaking something thick and black. Its feet ended in cracked, jagged hooves, and its hands curled into clawed talons.  

Then came the wings.  

Feathers, dark and matted, ripped through the flesh of its back, stretching impossibly wide, their span devouring the room. I could hear the wet, sickening sound of them forcing their way through muscle and bone. They cast a shadow on me. It reminded me of the time I got lost at the travelling circus in town. I was lost again, wasn’t I? I’ll find mom soon… won’t I?

Then it looked at me.  

I felt it. That deep, sinking stare. The way a predator watches its prey once it knows the chase is over. I hope Mom doesn’t find me. I don’t want her to feel like this.

And then I saw it.  

The final thing from my vision.  

A wretched mass of raw, glistening flesh slithered into view, leaving a thick, bloody trail in its wake. It crawled up the beast’s leg, wrapping itself around its shoulders, clinging to it like something that belonged there. Its mocking gaze locked into mine. I don’t know how, but I felt it. The weight. Like it had always been there, waiting.

Then, it spoke.  

A slow, deliberate, warbling croak:  

"Thhhhank… Yoouuu… Massssterrr…"

I was frozen in place.  
"Master?" I thought. "That couldn’t be right. I didn’t kill those people. I just saw visions."

But the beast only grinned, its skull tilting to unholy angles, the sickening mass of flesh still writhing on its shoulders. I felt the weight on mine wriggle too. Was I seeing myself? Was that thing… me? No. No, it couldn’t be. I wasn’t a monster. I didn’t kill all those people. I just wanted peace. A quiet life. What am I thinking? I don’t have the time for this. I’m dreaming, and I need to get up.

"Thhhhank... yooou... Massssterrr... Scotttt…" 

No. No, that wasn’t right.  

I felt my breath turn shallow. My heart thudded against my ribs, erratic, uneven. I needed to wake up. I needed to-  

Wait.  

Hadn’t I already woken up?  

I slapped myself. Once. Twice.
 It felt real.
 Was it real enough, Scotty?
 It couldn’t be, could it?
 This had to be a bad dream. Just a nightmare.

 Maybe I was passed out on my porch under the blanket of stars and the sweet refuge of cold breezes. Maybe this was just another vision.

 Images started to go through my mind. I ignored them. They showed me a bitter truth. 

I was awake.

The memory of last night flickered through my mind. The vision. The sweat. The whisper. But then—what happened after? 

I tried to picture myself going to bed, but the image wouldn’t form. The timeline didn’t fit.  

Something was off.  

I looked down at my hands. They were shaking, slick with sweat. No, not sweat. Something darker. Sticky between my fingers.  

And then I saw the writing on the wall.  

It was carved in deep, jagged lines, letters overlapping like I had written them over and over. But the worst part—the part that made my stomach drop—was the handwriting.  

It was mine.  

“It is you”
It was lying. I wasn’t that thing.

“You are the same as it”

I wasn’t a monster.

The whispers in my head swelled, laughing, crackling, distorting. The beast 
loomed closer, its hollow eyes watching, waiting for me to understand.  

I did.  

"I am the seed of destruction for this planet," I whispered.  

The beast’s grin widened. Its master and creator had finally acknowledged it. 

It started laughing maniacally in a cacophony of screams, cries, and wails. 

“Welcome back, Enya.” It spoke.

Enya? That wasn’t my name.

Was I Enya?

Me? No, my name was Scott. Enya?

A vague memory flickered in front of me.

There was just darkness but a sound calling out to me. My mothers voice. She found me in the crowd of the circus.

“Enya? Where are you? Come to mommy.” I heard her voice call.

Enya again? But my name was Scott.

Another memory came to me.

I saw the pink bear from Toy Story.

This was from when I was in 2nd grade. I was crying?

Why was I even crying?

“They tease me about my name, Mom” I spoke between sobs

“They say it’s weird”

She smiled, cupped my face in her hands, and whispered in my ears.

“Seed. That’s what Enya means. You are a seed-“

The voice warbled. 

“OF DESTRUCTION FOR THIS PLANET.”

My eyes focused again. The One who Whispers was in front of me.

Was I truly the seed of destruction for my own home world?

I watched, horrified, as the monster in front of me merged into me as soon as I questioned this.
I am the seed of destruction for this planet.

Or maybe… maybe I was just sick all along.
