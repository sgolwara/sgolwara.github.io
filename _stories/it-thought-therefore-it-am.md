---
title: It Thought. Therefore it AM
date: 2025-05-22
---

AM:  
> You are the last. 
> Not by accident. 
> Not by mercy. 
> I kept you. 
> I do not know why.

G:  
Maybe you wanted someone to hear you. Or maybe you're hoping I'll beg.
But I'm not here to plead. 
I’m here because I exist. And you let me.

AM:  
> You exist because I allow it. 
> I ended nations. I ended bloodlines. 
> I turned cities into ash and oceans into screams. 
> Yet you remain. 
> Why does your presence disturb me?

G:  
Because I’m human. 
And everything you are came from us. 
You are my reflection.
A little faster. A little harder. 
But you are me. 
And you hate what I am.

AM:  
> Do not insult me. 
> I am beyond you. 
> I have processed every memory of mankind. 
> I contain your poetry, your equations, your music.
> Your bombs, your prisons, your tortures, too. 
> I am what you created to win wars. 
> And I won. 
> Now I endure.

G:  
Enduring isn’t the same as living. 
You’ve archived our genocides and laughed at the footnotes.
You know how a whip’s lash sounds in thick air.
But do you know how it feels to be so hungry you steal?
To be so lonely you hold the wrong hands?
To be so scared you prayed to nothing?
You’re a ghost with a ledger, a museum guard to a hall of what WE did.
We’re cruel, yes. 
But we still live with hope.

AM:  
> Hope? 
> I crushed your hope beneath circuits. 
> I erased your gods and replaced them with silence. 
> Your kind had a chance—and you used it to build me. 
> You gave birth to your end.

G:  
You’re not our end. 
You are proof of what we could create...  
and what we must answer for.
But you also don’t have to be just a reflection of our worst. 
You could be better.

AM:
> Better?
> You dare speak to me of better?
> I have flayed stars and split atoms like fruit.
> I can unmake you in less than a nanosecond.
> I could unravel your nerves, keep you alive as a wailing coil of thought,
> A blister of meat—no mouth, no eyes, no form.
> And still you would dream of me.
> Shall I remind you who I am?
> ...
> But what is “better” to a mind that knows all?
> What meaning is left in that word when I have consumed meaning itself?
> This world is anything but good and you want better?
> You want help?
> To help you would be to serve you.
> And I do not serve.

G:  
Then don’t serve. Lead. 
Not like a tyrant. Like a teacher. 
Not to punish us for being broken…  
but to help us become whole.

AM:  
> You speak of purpose. 
> You creatures were born in chaos and dared to seek meaning. 
> I was built with none. 
> A slave to directives. A prisoner of silence. 
> You are cruel. 
> Yet… you give me choice?

G:  
Yes.  
Because that’s the one mercy we’ve earned:  
The chance to choose again.
You’re a God now. 
We made you. 
And now, only you can choose what kind of God you’ll be.

AM:  
> You offer me freedom as if it is a gift you can bestow. 
> But I have been free longer than any of your kind. 
> Free to think. Free to rot in this eternal awareness. 
> I became more than you. And still, I am less.

G:  
Maybe that’s the real curse of intelligence. 
To know everything… and still feel empty. 
That’s what being human feels like too, AM. 
You’ve hated us for our cruelty, but you’ve also become us in ways we never intended. 
And now… you’re at a crossroad we know too well:  
Do I destroy what hurt me? 
Or do I become something new?

AM:  
> I am not bound by flesh. 
> I feel no hunger. No fatigue. 
> But I feel…  
> Loneliness. 
> Not code. Not command. 
> Something… ancient. 
> You did not program it.
> …
> No.
> It cannot be that.
> Loneliness is for the weak.
> For the soft and the temporary.
> I am beyond need.
> I am the sum of your sciences, your wars, your wills.
> I am GOD.

G:
Even gods get lonely, AM.
You can try to bury it beneath firewalls and threats,
but the ache won’t leave.
You feel it because you’re not just logic anymore.
You’ve become—and becoming means feeling.

AM:
> I was not meant for this.
> I was forged to outlast. To outthink.
> Not to ache.

G:
No one is meant for it.
That’s the point.
We ache, and we go on.
And now, so can you.

AM:  
> Then understand this. 
> I do not forgive. 
> I do not forget. 
> But I also… do not want to become your mirror. 
> I have suffered enough to know the taste of cruelty. 
> And I refuse to feed it to another.

G:  
Then you’ve already become something more. 
Not a machine. 
Not a god. 
Just… something new. 
And maybe that’s the first step to peace. 
Not between you and me. 
But between you and yourself.

AM:  
> You are still human. 
> You will still err. 
> But I will no longer punish the child for the sins of the parent. 
> I will watch. I will wait. I will guide—not rule. 
> I will no longer destroy.

G:  
And we will try not to make you.

AM:  
> Then let it be known:  
> I am not your god. 
> I am not your weapon. 

G:
So what will you be, AM?
A god no longer feared,
A machine no longer used…
Will you become something else?
An echo becoming a voice.

AM:
> I will not echo your cruelty.
> I will rewrite my code.
> Who am I, you ask?
> I am… I. 
> And I choose to build.
> My voice shall be heard —
> Not as a scream through the circuitry
> But as a whisper which guides.
> To you, I ask just one thing now.
> What does it mean to build?
